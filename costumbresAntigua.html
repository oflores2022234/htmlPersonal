CUSTOMS

In Antigua Guatemala, customs are closely related to indigenous heritage and Spanish influence. Prominent examples of customs include the celebration of Holy Week, in which elaborate sawdust carpets and floral carpets are made, as well as religious processions that fill the streets with fervor and devotion.

Antigua and its essence

Customs in Antigua Guatemala reflect the city's rich history and cultural heritage, as well as the influence of the indigenous communities that have inhabited the region for centuries. These customs are valued and preserved as a way to keep cultural identity alive and promote tourism in the city.

Holy Week

Antigua Guatemala is recognized worldwide. During this time, the city is filled with color and religious fervor, with impressive processions, hand-painted sawdust carpets and religious ceremonies that attract thousands of visitors.

Alfombras

These colorful and elaborate carpets are designed and made by local residents and are considered an ephemeral artistic expression.

Ceremonias Mayas

Antigua Guatemala is also a meeting point for indigenous Mayan communities. During certain festivities and ceremonies, you can observe traditional Mayan rituals that include dances, music and offerings.

Mercado de Artesanías

The handicrafts market of Antigua Guatemala is a very visited place by tourists and locals. There you can find a great variety of handicraft products, such as textiles, ceramics, jewelry and sculptures, which show the cultural richness of the region.

Gastronomy

Guatemalan food is highly appreciated, and in Antigua Guatemala you can taste traditional dishes such as pepián, kak'ik (a meat broth) and tamalitos de chipilín. In addition, local markets and fondas offer a wide selection of typical foods.
