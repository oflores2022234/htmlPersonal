Places In Antigua

Antigua Guatemala offers a wide variety of tourist attractions. Art lovers can visit numerous galleries and museums, where works of art and traditional crafts are exhibited. There are also antique stores, jewelry stores and stores selling local products, where visitors can purchase unique souvenirs.

Which places can you visit?

The surroundings of Antigua Guatemala also offer opportunities to enjoy the natural beauty of the region. The Agua, Fuego and Acatenango volcanoes are located near the city and offer breathtaking panoramic views. In addition, you can visit Lake Atitlán, one of the most beautiful lakes in the world, and explore the surrounding indigenous villages.

Catedral de Santiago

Located in the Central Park of Antigua Guatemala, the Cathedral of Santiago is one of the main points of interest of the city. Built in the 16th century, this imposing Catholic church presents a mixture of Baroque and Renaissance architectural styles.

Arco de Santa Catalina

The Arch of Santa Catalina is one of the most photographed places in Antigua Guatemala. Built in the seventeenth century, the arch served as a gateway to the convent of Santa Catalina.

Ruinas de la Iglesia y Convento de las Capuchinas

These ruins are a fascinating testimony to the history of Antigua Guatemala. The convent was built in the 18th century and housed nuns of the Capuchin order.

Convento de Santo Domingo

The Santo Domingo Convent is another outstanding historical and tourist site in Antigua. Founded in the 16th century, this convent was one of the largest and wealthiest in the city in its time. Today, it houses the Hotel Casa Santo Domingo, a luxury hotel that preserves much of the original architecture of the convent.

Mercado de Artesanías

For lovers of shopping and traditional handicrafts, Antigua Guatemala's Mercado de Artesanías is a must-see. Located in the center of the city, this market is a treasure trove of color and creativity. Here you will find a wide variety of handmade products made by local artisans, such as textiles, ceramics, jewelry, leather and much more.
