Coffee in Antigua

Antigua Guatemala Coffee refers to a variety of coffee grown in the region of Antigua, located in the department of Sacatepéquez, in Guatemala. This region is known for its long tradition in the production of high quality coffee.

The elixir of coffee

Antigua Guatemala Coffee is characterized by its distinctive and complex flavor, combining sweet, fruity and floral notes. The nutrient-rich volcanic soils, ideal climate and high altitude at which the coffee is grown contribute to its exceptional flavor profile.

High altitude coffee

This coffee is grown in the mountainous areas and on the slopes of the volcanoes of t he Antigua region. The altitude at which it is grown varies between 1,200 and 1,800 meters above sea level.

Shade coffee

This type of coffee is grown under the shade of taller trees, which provides natural protection from direct sunlight. Shade cultivation contributes to a slower and more gradual development of the beans.

Antigua Guatemala Coffee has gained international recognition and is highly appreciated by coffee lovers around the world. Its superior quality and refined flavor profile make this coffee a unique and delicious experience for those who have the opportunity to taste it.

Cultivation

Coffee cultivation in Antigua is mainly carried out using traditional and sustainable methods. Farmers in the region use careful cultivation techniques, such as hand-picking of ripe beans and the implementation of shade practices, which protect the coffee trees from direct sunlight and promote biodiversity in the coffee plantations.

Process

The process begins with the sowing of seeds in nurseries or directly in the fields. The seeds are carefully selected and planted in soils rich in nutrients, characteristic of the volcanic region of Antigua. The coffee plantations are cultivated at altitudes that vary between 1,200 and 1,800 meters above sea level, taking advantage of the ideal climatic conditions of the area.
