Antigua Guatemala

Antigua Guatemala, also known as Antigua, is a historic city located in the deparment of Sacatepequez in Guatemala. It was founded in 1543 as Santiago de los Caballeros of Guatemala and served as the capital of the Kingdom of Guatemala. It comprised the current territories of Guatemala, Belize, El Salvador, Honduras, Nicaragua and Costa Rica.

Customs

In Antigua Guatemala, customs are closely related to indigenous heritage and Spanish influence. Prominent examples of customs include the celebration of Holy Week, in which elaborate sawdust carpets and floral carpets are made, as well as religious processions that fill the streets with fervor and devotion.

Traditions

In Antigua Guatemala, traditions are rooted in the rich history and fusion of indigenous and Spanish cultures. These traditions encompass various aspects of daily life and special events, and are expressed in areas such as religion, crafts, music, dance and gastronomy.
