History

Antigua Guatemala is a colonial city located in the heart of Central America. It was founded in 1543 and became the capital of the Kingdom of Guatemala of Guatemala during the Spanish colonial period.

Foundation and Colony

Antigua Guatemala was founded on March 10, 1543 by the Spanish conquistador Pedro de Alvarado. It was established as the third capital of Guatemala, after Iximche and Ciudad Vieja.

Pedro de Alvarado

Pedro de Alvarado was an outstanding Spanish conquistador born in 1485 in the town of Badajoz, Spain. He is recognized for his participation in the conquest of Central America during the XVI century.

Iximche was an ancient Maya city and capital of the Kaqchikel kingdom, located in Tecpan, Guatemala. It was founded around 1463 and became an important center.

Ciudad Vieja

It was founded by the conquistador Pedro de Alvarado in 1527 and was established in the Almolonga Valley, near Antigua Guatemala.

Architecture

The stunning architectural beauty of Antigua Guatemala. Antigua Guatemala has a inpressive architecture that combines Spanish, indigenous and baroque elements. Its streets are full of colonial houses.

Heritage

Highlights include monuments such as the Cathedral of Santiago, the Palace of the Captains General, the church and Convent of La Merced, the church and convent of Santo Domingo.
