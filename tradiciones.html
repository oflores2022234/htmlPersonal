TRADITIONS

In Antigua Guatemala, traditions are rooted in the rich history and fusion of indigenous and Spanish cultures. These traditions encompass various aspects of daily life and special events, and are expressed in areas such as religion, crafts, music, dance and gastronomy.

ANTIGUA AND ITS ESSENCE

The traditions of Antigua Guatemala are the cultural practices and festivities rooted in the daily life of the city. These traditions reflect the history, heritage and customs of the region. Here are some of the most outstanding traditions of Antigua Guatemala:

Fiestas Patronales

In Antigua Guatemala, the patron saint festivities are held in honor of Santo Tomás and Santiago. These festivities include processions, masses, music, folkloric dances, mechanical games and a variety of cultural activities.

Pyrotechnics Games

Impressive light and sound shows can be witnessed during religious events, festivals and holidays.

Night of the Burning of the Bull

This tradition takes place on December 7, on the eve of the Day of the Virgin of the Immaculate Conception. During the night, a papier-mâché bull filled with fireworks is built, which is then lit and launched into the air, creating a pyrotechnic spectacle.

Burning of the Devil

This tradition takes place on December 7, the eve of the Day of the Virgin of the Immaculate Conception. On this date, figures and piñatas representing the devil are burned and bonfires are held to cleanse homes of bad energies before the Christmas festivities.

Folkloric Dances

Traditional dance and music have an important place in the culture of Antigua Guatemala. Guatemalan folk dances, such as the Baile de la Conquista and the Baile de los Moros, are performed during various festivities and cultural events.
